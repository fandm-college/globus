# F&M Globus User Guide
{: .fs-9 }

## Move, share, and manage research data securely.
{: .fs-6 .fw-300 }

Globus is the Research Computing Services recommended platform for transferring large research datasets, sharing data with collaborators, and accessing research storage across institutions.

Whether you are moving data between your laptop and research storage or collaborating with researchers at another institution, Globus provides reliable, secure, and fault-tolerant file transfers.

![Diagram showing Globus connecting laptops, research storage, HPC storage, and collaborators]({{ site.baseurl }}/assets/images/diagrams/globus-workflow.svg){: .figure }

## At a glance

- Transfer large datasets reliably.
- Resume interrupted transfers automatically.
- Share research data securely.
- Collaborate across institutions.
- Monitor transfers from anywhere.

## Is Globus right for me?

| If you want to... | Recommended tool |
|:---|:---|
| Transfer a 100 GB research dataset | **Globus** |
| Move data between institutions | **Globus** |
| Share research data securely | **Globus** |
| Resume interrupted transfers | **Globus** |
| Edit documents collaboratively | **Google Drive / Google Docs** |
| Email a small PDF | **Email** |

{: .recommendation }
> If your research data is larger than a few gigabytes, or if a transfer might take more than a few minutes, Globus is almost always a better choice than downloading files through a web browser or attaching files to email.

## Looking for more?

This guide focuses on the Globus features most commonly used by the Franklin & Marshall research community. For advanced configuration options or full product documentation, see the [official Globus documentation](https://docs.globus.org/).
